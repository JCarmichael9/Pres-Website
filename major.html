Civil Rights Act of 1964

Lyndon B. Johnson signed the Civil Rights Act of 1964, which outlawed segregation and discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.

Great Society Programs

He launched the Great Society programs, including Medicare and Medicaid, to reduce poverty and improve healthcare for Americans.

Voting Rights Act of 1965

Johnson passed the Voting Rights Act of 1965, eliminating barriers like literacy tests that prevented Black Americans from voting.
