Johnson's Early Life
Lyndon B. Johnson was born on August 27, 1908, in Stonewall, Texas.
Johnson's father, Samuel Ealy Johnson Jr., was a farmer and congressman, and his mother, Rebekah Baines Johnson, was an educated woman who supported Lyndon's early ambitions, with two older brothers, Sam Houston Johnson and Reuben Baines Johnson, and a politically active lower-middle-class family.
Johnson attended Southwest Texas State Teachers College (now Texas State University) in San Marcos, Texas, where he earned a degree in 1930, focusing on teaching but with a passion for public service and politics.
Growing up in poverty, Johnson developed empathy for the poor, which shaped his focus on civil rights and social welfare programs, and his teaching experience in Cotulla influenced his views on education and equality.
